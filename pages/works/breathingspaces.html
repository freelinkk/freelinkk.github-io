Breathing Spaces
Performance & Installation
Shown at Zone2Source, part of the Sonic Acts Biennale
March, 2024

Breathing Spaces was the collective work and culmination of the Spatial Sounds Workshop from Sonic Acts as part of their 2024 Bienalle: The Spell of the Sensuous. The workshop spanned 2 months and involved lectures, hands-on engagement, and practise with acoustic feedback, field recording, experimental recording techniques, an 8 channel surround system, Supercollider, resonance, and reverberation to name a few. The goal of the workshop was to create an installation and/or performative piece which would be presented to a public on the 17th March. The end collective process was called Breathing Spaces, where each member, either individually or in a small group, presented their piece one by one in a single continuous performance flow, without break; extending beyond physical boundaries and incorporating online realms, where we sonically shared our discoveries on audio feedback, ambience, and the relationship between spatial trajectories and sound phenomena. After each individual moment, we all collectively improvised and performed together based on a pre-determined flow, but undetermined and unexpected sonic aesthetic, testing the listening and knowledge we had developed throughout the workshop. The name of the installation I created with Atser Damsma was called Living Room.

The workshop was hosted at Zone2Source, an art gallery centered around the themes of art & ecology located in the Amstelpark, Amsterdam.


Living Room
Jason Andrew Clark & Atser Damsma

This installation investigates whether a space can be heard as an organism: alive, with its own inherent bodily rhythms and resonances. The corners of the room are connected through a dialogue between two sets of microphones and speakers, creating a feedback loop in which space is collapsed into itself. The resulting resonance not only reflects the natural breath of the room, but also mirrors, enlarges, and interacts with everything that happens inside; where objects, humans, and sounds are its entangled organs.



Spatial Sound Workshop - Sonic Acts Description

When we engage with sound, we are confronted with its interconnected relationship to space. Reverberation, refraction, and reflection not only communicate characteristics about sound itself, but also offer insight into the surrounding environment. This shared synergy is fundamental to our daily existence and interactions. Taking up novel perspectives, the Spatial Sound Workshop – with its host, Ji Youn Kang – will examine the role of these phenomena in shaping sound propagation. Can a diverse array of technologies help us unravel the intricate connections between sound, space, and our sensory experience?

In this site-specific workshop, participants will embark on an exploration of two distinct spaces in profound detail, harnessing their potential as unconventional musical instruments. The scope extends beyond the boundaries of the physical, delving into the possibilities of online realms. In both physical and virtual contexts, various tools such as microphones, loudspeakers, and some basic programming will be utilised to discover the voices of these spaces and allow them to ‘sing together’. The culmination of the workshop will be a sound piece, or a concert, presented at Zone2Source with all participants that encapsulates the essence of the collective experiments.
