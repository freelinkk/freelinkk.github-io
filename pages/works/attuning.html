Attuning our Bodies, on view from 22 May to 15 June 2025, is the next iteration of the ongoing exhibition series Sensory Dialogues, organised by Treehouse NDSM. This show is concerned with the multisensorial process of the body, what we can learn from the embodied experience of others, and how these practices and experiences can be shared. As the name of the exhibition suggests, the featured artworks require a process of attuning; to employ a sense of play and exploration with our own bodies to find how and when we resonate with the artworks and their stories.

Some of the artworks create critical spaces which help us negotiate the ever-growing technological systems which permeate our lives. SA900_Port by Christian Schwarz offers a form of data sonification by collecting information from nearby phones and translating it into a sound composition. How does this renegotiate our understanding of the phones in our pocket after coming to terms with their expansive sonic capabilities? Marlot Meyer’s Sensitive Systems is an interactive experience which places the listener in situations of ‘being with’ technology, challenging our normative perceptions of technology as solely binary and quantitative. This project will be presented primarily as a workshop. Franz Ehn’s LLLAAA is an interactive silicon mouthpiece which materialises the functionality of speech synthesis and text-to-speech, therefore offering an insight into how these complex systems function. LLLAAA nicely intersects with another strand of the exhibition, namely the depth that multisensorial listening offers to understand our own bodily processes. Necrosonic Landscapes by Berkveldt is an installation that gives an insight into how the process of death and decomposition is not silent but full of sound and life. Bone Conductor by Ricardo Huisman is an interactive installation, a large woollen sound bone, which you are invited to embrace and listen to closely. It feeds the ear with sound and vibrations, offers a personal exploration into our own hearing experiences and how we absorb and resonate with sound vibrations.

To wrap the exhibition in a sonic blanket; Jason Clark & Xico Ribas present a site-specific installation: Subject to the sonic address of an unknown source. This installation is hidden within the walls of the exhibition space, aiming to bring about a meta-reflection in its destabilisation of how we listen to and engage with exhibitions, and more specifically how we listen to Attuning our Bodies. Bringing to the forefront the audience’s positionality is also an important poin=t for Sofia Fernandez Blanco’s Black Box; where seven black boxes offer earwitness listening experiences of inaccessible public spaces; places where freedom of movement or entry are either partially or completely denied. These soundscapes gain new significance in how we listen to the persistence of life and community in these contexts.

An essential aspect of Attuning the Ears is its integration of workshops and performances. The theme demands the creation of spaces for active and momentary listening, allowing for the practice of improvisational works. It also gives a unique possibility of engaging in the communal and ritualistic experiences that allow for a more immediate understanding of embodied knowledge and how it has historically been shared. Marko Ivic will present Reflective Music, an improvisation-based practice which invites the audience into a state of individual and close listening. Echoes of the Unheard is a multisensory performance by Tania Zountsa that primarily explores the power of silence, while at the same time invites the audience to listen differently, to connect more deeply with the unseen and unheard aspects of our inner and outer worlds. NOTATIONS by Robin Becker is a collective group piece which will start as a listening meditation and move into a vocal meditation, slowly unfolding into a free vocal improvisation. At this final point, the participants are invited to notate their singing graphically. The resulting piece will be hung and presented during the exhibition. Additionally, Ricardo Huisman will extend the boundaries of his work The Bone Conductor, adding extra vibrational soundscapes and layers, and interacting with the public as they engage with the installation. Jason Clark & Xico Ribas will reveal their installation by pushing it to its upper and lower limits, revealing how cracks and leaks show the potential of the exhibition space as an instrument.

This exhibition seeks to uplift and support the importance of collective and individual embodied practices of knowledge. Not necessarily as an escape or antidote to modern living, but as a companion; to create spaces of solidarity which allow for our emotional experiences to bring us closer together.
