Subject to the sonic address of an unknown source
Performance & Installation
Jason Clark & Xico Ribas
Shown at Treehouse NDSM, part of the Sensory Dialogues: Attuning Our Bodies group exhibition
May - June, 2025

Subject to the sonic address of an unknown source is an on going research project by Xico Ribas and I. It comes from a place of curiosity, to triggers a change in the audience’s listening awareness without directly addressing them. This site specific installation aims to create an instability of how we listen, and how we sonically engage and participate in public and private spaces. This is achieved by encouraging sonic leakages and augmentations from both the inside and outside of the pavilion. Our expected perception of the space is subtly transformed. The first part of this project was done in the form of an installation, where placed different microphones and transducers throughout a warehouse space, which we use to relocalise/contextualise live sounds. This set-up allowed us to experiment with its apparent size, as well as its materiality and/or tonal balance. We also used this installation setup to perform in front of an audience, and explore through ourselves the possibility of an entire space as an instrument.
